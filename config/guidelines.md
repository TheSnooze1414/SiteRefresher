# Copy Guidelines

## Voice & Tone
- Confident, direct, and benefit-led — lead with what the customer gains
- Avoid jargon; if a technical term is used, follow it with a plain-language payoff
- Active voice preferred; minimize passive constructions
- Conversational but authoritative — not casual, not stiff

## Style Rules
- Headline case for headings (capitalize major words)
- Em dash (—) not hyphen for breaks in copy
- Numerals for specs (3.5L, 400 hp, 0–60 in 4.2 sec)
- Spell out numbers one through nine in body copy
- No exclamation points in body copy; sparingly in headlines only

## Spec Mentions
- Always state the new spec value clearly and accurately
- If the old value is mentioned anywhere, remove or replace it
- Pair spec values with a benefit statement where space allows
  - Example: "3.5L EcoBoost V6 — 400 horsepower delivered with improved fuel economy"

## Forbidden Phrases
- "Best-in-class" (unless backed by a footnote)
- "Revolutionary" / "game-changing"
- "State-of-the-art"
- "Industry-leading" (unless footnoted)
- Starting a sentence with "We are pleased to announce"

## Length Guidance
- Headlines: 4–10 words
- Feature callouts / bullets: 10–20 words
- Body paragraphs: 2–4 sentences; avoid walls of text
- Preserve approximate length of the original segment when rewriting
